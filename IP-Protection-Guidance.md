# DIVE Framework™ — Intellectual Property Protection Guidance

**Purpose:** This document provides a step-by-step roadmap for legally protecting the DIVE Framework as your intellectual property. It covers copyright, trademark, defensive publication, and strategic considerations.

---

## 1. Overview of Protection Strategies

| Mechanism | What It Protects | Duration | Cost | Geographic Scope |
|-----------|-----------------|----------|------|-----------------|
| **Copyright** | Written expression (documents, templates, code) | Author's life + 70 years | ~$35-65 per registration | Global (Berne Convention) |
| **Trademark** | Name, logo, branding elements | Indefinite (renew every 10 years) | ~$250-500 per class per country | Jurisdiction-specific |
| **Defensive Publication** | Prior art — prevents others from patenting | N/A (establishes date of public disclosure) | $0-500 | Global |
| **Trade Secret** | Confidential methodologies, processes | Indefinite (as long as secret is maintained) | Cost of NDAs/security | Global |
| **Patent** (optional) | Novel processes or inventions | 20 years | $5,000-50,000+ | Jurisdiction-specific |

> **Recommendation:** For a project management methodology, copyright + trademark + defensive publication is the most cost-effective and enforceable combination. Patent protection is generally not appropriate for business methods in many jurisdictions.

## 2. Step-by-Step Protection Plan

### Step 1: Establish Priority of Creation (Do This First)

| Action | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Date-stamp all files** | Ensure all documents have creation dates in metadata |
| **Save dated evidence** | Store original files with timestamps in a secure, immutable location |
| **Document the origin story** | Write a brief narrative of: when you conceived the framework, key milestones, development process |
| **Witnessed record** | Have a trusted third party sign and date a statement confirming your authorship |

### Step 2: Copyright Registration

| Item | Instructions |
|------|-------------|
| **What to register** | The complete framework document (this methodology reference) AND each individual template as a collection |
| **Where to register** | Your national copyright office (e.g., US Copyright Office at copyright.gov, UK Copyright Service, etc.) |
| **Process** | 1. Compile all documents into a single PDF<br>2. Complete application form<br>3. Pay filing fee (~$35-65)<br>4. Deposit copy of work<br>5. Receive certificate (3-12 months) |
| **What copyright covers** | The specific written expression — text of principles, lifecycle descriptions, template wording, governance policies |
| **What copyright does NOT cover** | Ideas, concepts, processes (these are protected differently) |

**Copyright notice to include on all documents:**

```
DIVE Framework™ — Copyright © [Year] Vahid Hakami. All rights reserved.
```

### Step 3: Trademark Registration

| Item | Instructions |
|------|-------------|
| **What to trademark** | "DIVE Framework" (name) and the DIVE logo (design) |
| **Classes to file under** | Class 9 (software), Class 41 (education/training), Class 42 (scientific/technological services) |
| **Where to register** | Start with your home country's trademark office:<br>• USPTO (uspto.gov) — US<br>• EUIPO (euipo.europa.eu) — EU<br>• WIPO (wipo.int) — International |
| **Search first** | Run a trademark search to ensure no conflicts exist with existing marks |
| **Process** | 1. Conduct clearance search<br>2. File application (use-based or intent-to-use)<br>3. Examination (3-6 months)<br>4. Publication for opposition (30 days)<br>5. Registration (if no opposition) |
| **Cost** | ~$250-500 per class per country |
| **Renewal** | Every 10 years (with proof of continued use at year 5-6) |

**Trademark notice format:**

- **Unregistered:** `DIVE Framework™` (™ for goods/services)
- **Registered:** `DIVE Framework®` (® only after official registration)

### Step 4: Defensive Publication

| Item | Instructions |
|------|-------------|
| **Purpose** | Defensive publication establishes that the DIVE Framework existed before anyone else might try to patent it. This creates "prior art" that prevents others from obtaining a patent on your invention. |
| **Where to publish** | • arXiv.org (free, indexed, timestamped)<br>• SSRN (Social Science Research Network)<br>• Medium / LinkedIn (less formal but timestamped)<br>• Your personal website with clear publication date<br>• GitHub repository with public commit history |
| **What to publish** | A summary white paper covering:<br>• Framework name and author<br>• Date of creation<br>• 7 core principles<br>• Lifecycle overview<br>• Novelty statement (what makes it different from PMBOK, PRINCE2, Agile, CPMAI) |
| **When to publish** | Before presenting to potential investors, partners, or licensees |
| **License choice** | Consider CC BY-ND (Attribution-NoDerivs) — allows sharing with attribution, prevents derivative works |

### Step 5: Trade Secret Protection (Optional)

For any proprietary details you choose NOT to publish:

| Action | Implementation |
|--------|---------------|
| NDAs | Require non-disclosure agreements before sharing detailed templates |
| Access control | Restrict access to full methodology documentation |
| Marking | Label confidential documents: "CONFIDENTIAL — DIVE Framework Proprietary Material" |
| Employee/IP agreements | Ensure all contributors assign IP rights to you |

## 3. Enforcement Strategy

| Scenario | Response |
|----------|---------|
| Someone uses the DIVE name without permission | Send cease & desist letter citing trademark rights |
| Someone copies templates verbatim | Send takedown notice citing copyright infringement (DMCA if online) |
| Someone claims to have invented a substantially similar framework | Reference defensive publication as prior art |
| A company wants to use the framework internally | Offer a license — per-seat, per-project, or enterprise |
